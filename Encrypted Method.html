4.Encrypt method

HTTPS uses a Transport Layer Security (TLS)/ Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) to encrypt messages between a client and server. (Awati, 2022) There are two primary types of Encryption which are symmetric encryptions and asymmetric encryptions. Symmetric encryption uses the same key for both the encryption and decryption of a message, while asymmetric encryption applies a public key and a private key to perform the encryption and decryption. (José,2023) Both encryption methods have advantages and disadvantages as mentioned below:

Symmetric encryptions Asymmetric encryptions
Advantage
  • Faster speed and simple use: It requires a single key to encrypt. (José,2023)
  • Higher efficiency: Significant time and cost savings can be achieved by adopting less processing power and resources for encryption and decryption. (José,2023)
  • Higher compatibility: It has widespread adoption and support of software and hardware platforms to ensure seamless integration with current applications and systems, often without requiring significant modifications. (José,2023)
  • Higher security: The private key used for decryption can reject unauthorized access to the data. (José,2023)
  • Higher flexibility: It offers widespread versatility, making it suitable for various applications. (José,2023)
Disadvantages
  • Lower security: Lower security: It has the possibility to have the risk of compromised keys, which could expose all encrypted messages, Then, interception by anyone with access to the key is possible. (Rossevelt,2023)
  • Slower and complex to use:It requires computationally intensive processes, longer key lengths, and complex algorithms, which may slow down networks (Gitlan,2024)
